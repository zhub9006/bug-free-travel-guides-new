# Bug-Free Travel Destination Guides

Welcome, bug-phobic travelers! Here are 5 researched destinations known for being exceptionally low in bugs, mosquitoes, and ticks — along with the safest seasons to visit each one.

## 1. Iceland (Bug Level: ZERO) — Best: Jun-Aug
No mosquitoes, no ticks. Cold volcanic island in the North Atlantic makes it inhospitable for insects.
- **Temperatures:** 10–15C (50–59F)
- **Key activities:** Golden Circle, Blue Lagoon, glacier hiking, whale watching
- **Tip:** Avoid still water at dusk in the far east; strong wind keeps any stray insects away

## 2. Hawaii (Bug Level: LOW) — Best: Apr-Jun
Island isolation means far fewer insects than mainland tropics. Trade winds keep bugs dispersed.
- **Temperatures:** 24–29C (75–85F)
- **Key activities:** Road to Hana, Volcanoes National Park, snorkeling, beaches
- **Tip:** Stay on the leeward (dry/west) side where mosquito activity is lowest; choose A/C/screened accommodations

## 3. New Zealand (Bug Level: VERY LOW) — Best: Dec-Feb
No snakes, no malaria, strict biosecurity prevents insect invasions. Almost entirely bug-free.
- **Temperatures:** 20–30C (68–86F)
- **Key activities:** Milford Sound, Tongariro Alpine Crossing, Queenstown, wine regions
- **Tip:** Only sandflies near Fiordland/Nelson waterways — use DEET there; everywhere else is essentially bug-free

## 4. UAE / Dubai & Abu Dhabi (Bug Level: VERY LOW) — Best: Nov-Mar
Arid desert climate = very low insect populations. Everything is air-conditioned and pest-controlled.
- **Temperatures:** 20–30C (68–86F)
- **Key activities:** Burj Khalifa, Palm Jumeirah, desert safaris, Louvre Abu Dhabi
- **Tip:** Carry a small repellent as a precaution for rare stray mosquitoes; indoor attractions are fully A/C'd and bug-free

## 5. Scotland (Islands) (Bug Level: LOW) — Best: May-Sep
Orkney, Shetland, and Outer Hebrides have virtually no mosquitoes or ticks. North Atlantic winds keep insects dispersed.
- **Temperatures:** 12–18C (54–64F) in summer
- **Key activities:** Orkney Islands, Shetland Islands, coastal paths, Scottish Borders
- **Tip:** Stick to wind-exposed coastlines and hilltops — avoid sheltered Highland glens in July–August when midges are active at dawn/dusk

## Safe Season Quick Reference

| Destination | Best Season | Bug Level | Main Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Iceland | Jun–Aug | ZERO | Rare midges near east fjords at dusk |
| Hawaii | Apr–Jun | LOW | Windward coasts in wet season (Dec–Mar) |
| New Zealand | Dec–Feb | VERY LOW | Sandflies only near Fiordland waterways |
| UAE | Nov–Mar | VERY LOW | Occasional stray mosquitoes in summer |
| Scotland Islands | May–Sep | LOW | Midges in sheltered Highland glens (Jul–Aug) |
